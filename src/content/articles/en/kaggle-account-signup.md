> **In one line**: Go to [kaggle.com](https://www.kaggle.com), click Register, choose "Register with Google" to sign in with an existing Google account, then enter an SMS code to verify your phone — and you have a Kaggle account that can download datasets and run notebooks.

**Keywords**: Kaggle, sign up, Register, Sign in with Google, data science, Datasets, Notebooks, phone verification, Persona, SMS code

## Why do you need a Kaggle account?

Kaggle is the world's largest data-science and machine-learning community — it calls itself "The World's AI Proving Ground." With over 30 million members, you can:

- **Download ready-made datasets**: want to practice data analysis? There's a huge library of free data.
- **Run notebooks in the cloud**: no need to set up a local Python environment — open a browser and start coding and training models.
- **Enter competitions and take free courses**: from beginner to hands-on.

Plenty of "grab real data and practice AI" tutorials on LaunchDock start by sending you to Kaggle. Let's get your account set up first. It's free and takes about 5 minutes.

You just need a membership card (account) to walk in. And this card is free.

## Step 1: Go to the Kaggle homepage and click Register

Open [kaggle.com](https://www.kaggle.com). Top-right you'll see **Sign In** and a black **Register** button. First time here? Click **Register**.

The homepage also drops two quick buttons right in the middle: **Register with Google** and **Register with Email**.

![Kaggle homepage, the Register button and the central Register with Google](/images/articles/kaggle-account-signup/kaggle-homepage.png)

---

## Step 2: Choose a sign-in method

A **"Welcome!"** card pops up with two tabs: **Sign In** (existing account) and **Register**. Below it are four methods:

- **Sign in with Google** (recommended, fastest)
- **Sign in with Email**
- **Sign in with Facebook**
- **Sign in with Yahoo**

Google is the easiest — no new password to set, just one click. We'll use Google here.

![Kaggle's sign-in / register card with four login methods](/images/articles/kaggle-account-signup/kaggle-signin-options.png)

> <img src="/images/dock_head_s.png" alt="Duck Editor" width="24" style="vertical-align: middle;"> **In other words**: "Sign in with Google" is like using one transit card to get into every venue instead of buying a new card at each gate. Your Google account is the key; Kaggle only gets your name and email — never your Google password. Safe.

### 🚨 I'd rather set my own username and password, not link Google?

No problem — pick **Sign in with Email** for the classic "enter email, set password, confirm via email" flow. You'll just have one more password to remember. If you already use Google, linking it is the least hassle.

---

## Step 3: Authorize with Google

After picking Google, you land on Google's account chooser asking which Google account to continue with. Pick one, and Google shows a consent screen telling you **Kaggle will receive your "name and profile picture" and "email address"** — that's normal. Click **Continue** to authorize.

Once authorized, you go straight to the Kaggle dashboard with a **"Welcome, ...!"** greeting, your Login Streak, and stats (Datasets, Notebooks, Competitions…).

![The Kaggle dashboard after login, with the welcome message and account stats](/images/articles/kaggle-account-signup/kaggle-welcome-dashboard.png)

## Step 4: Phone verification to unlock full features

After login, Kaggle often shows: **"Your account is unverified"** and invites you to **Verify Your Account**.

Kaggle uses phone verification to block spam accounts and bots. Click it, and a third-party service called **Persona** takes over:

1. Enter your phone number (for Taiwan, choose +886).
2. Persona sends a 4-digit SMS code.
3. Type the 4 digits into the four boxes and click **Continue**.

![Persona phone verification screen, entering the 4-digit SMS code](/images/articles/kaggle-account-signup/kaggle-phone-verification.png)

Once verified, your Kaggle account is complete — you can download datasets, create notebooks, and enter competitions.

> <img src="/images/dock_head_s.png" alt="Duck Editor" width="24" style="vertical-align: middle;"> **Duck's note**: Phone verification is a bit like showing ID to get a library card — it's not to hassle you, it's to stop people from spinning up fake accounts to cause trouble. Verify once, and you're free to roam.

### 🚨 Not getting the SMS code?

- Check the country code (Taiwan mobiles use **+886**, and the leading 0 usually isn't typed again).
- Wait 1–2 minutes; SMS can be slow.
- Still nothing? There's usually a "resend" option on screen — tap it and try again.

## Done! What next?

You now have a working Kaggle account. Start with:

1. Browse **Datasets** and download something you find interesting (sales, weather, movie ratings).
2. Open a **Notebook** — Kaggle gives you a free cloud compute environment, no local Python needed.
3. When you have time, the free short courses under **Courses** are great for beginners.

Later, whenever a LaunchDock tutorial needs "real data to practice AI / data analysis," you'll be ready to follow along.
